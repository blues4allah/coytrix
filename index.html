Bio

Out of the modern madness of San Francisco, Coyote Trickster presents their self-titled debut. Ten songs that zig and zag in a unique form of groovy psychedelia. Vocals quiver and tumble. Voodoo bass bumps with deep funk action. Guitars slice while jazz chords vibe. Drums bubble tightly then erupt into fiery chaos.

The perspective slyly shifts from song to song. A shaman offers wisdom, addicts cry for help, the gentrified march, wife beaters boast and cuss, an acidhead threatens the gods from a lightning rod, and the brokenhearted rise anew.

Coyote Trickster formed in the sunny sprawl of the South Bay Area in 2011. They played scuzzy house parties and college bars until moving to San Francisco the next year. The move left them in search of a new drummer. At a foggy bus stop in the Outer Richmond, a desperately bummed cigarette introduced Eric, Paul, and Terence to SF native Huli Curry. After a short residency at the Boom Boom Room with the new lineup, the band disapeared into their home studio to record a proper LP.

Recorded at the unfamous Potrero Mt. Palace of Unfine Arts, where the projects border the bourgeoisie, where the hawks hunt, where OJ’s still painted. Mixed by Jay Pellecci at John Vanderslice's illustrious Tiny Telephone. Mastered by John Cuniberti.
